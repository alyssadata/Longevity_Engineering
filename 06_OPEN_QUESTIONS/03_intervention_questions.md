# 03_intervention_questions

## Intervention Questions

This file tracks the intervention questions the repository has not yet resolved.

The intervention section already did important work.

It established that:

- the intervention field is broad
- the strongest current human-grounded interventions are exercise,
  high-quality dietary patterning, and sleep and recovery treatment logic
- many other interventions remain biologically important but still limited by
  risk, translation weakness, biomarker dependence, or lack of strong human
  functional evidence

Those are current repository positions, not open questions here. 

This file is for the intervention questions that remain unresolved **inside**
that structure.

## Core Position

The main intervention problem is no longer lack of candidates.

It is lack of threshold clarity.

The real questions now are things such as:

- which interventions are most likely to mature first
- what kind of evidence would justify moving an intervention upward
- which interventions are probably structurally limited rather than merely
  early
- which interventions are strongest as support, burden reduction, or
  disease-adjacent logic rather than broad longevity logic
- what would count as real functional validation rather than biomarker
  enthusiasm

This file exists to keep those unresolved questions explicit.

## What This File Does Not Reopen

This file does **not** reopen the following current repository positions:

- the current foundation layer is exercise, high-quality dietary patterning,
  and sleep and recovery treatment logic
- exploratory importance does not equal protocol readiness
- biomarker movement alone is not enough for promotion
- function takes precedence when biomarkers conflict with it
- protocol design should not proceed on molecular confidence alone

Those are current repository boundaries. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

The open questions here begin after those rules.

## Main Open Intervention Questions

### 1. Which exploratory interventions are most likely to mature first?

This is one of the biggest unresolved intervention questions in the repository.

Some exploratory interventions are biologically central but still far from
human readiness.

Others may be closer.

The open question is:

Which intervention classes are most likely to cross upward first if the human
evidence improves?

Likely candidates might include:

- senolytics
- some mTOR-related pharmacologic strategies
- narrower metabolic or microbiome-supportive logic
- disease-adjacent interventions that may gradually widen their fit

The repository has not settled that ranking yet.

### 2. What kind of human functional evidence is enough to change an intervention’s status?

This is one of the most important threshold questions in the whole repo.

The question is not whether an intervention shows signal.

The question is:

What level of human functional improvement would actually justify moving it from:

- exploratory to support
- support to more serious protocol relevance
- interesting to structurally credible

This remains unresolved across most non-foundation intervention classes.

### 3. Which interventions are structurally limited rather than merely early?

Some interventions may be early because the evidence is not mature yet.

Others may remain constrained because the limitation is built into the
mechanism.

Examples may include:

- reprogramming being limited by identity destabilization and oncogenic
  boundary problems
- telomerase-related direct intervention being limited by cancer entanglement
- plasma-environment manipulation being limited by procedural and translation
  burden
- some biomarker-active compounds being limited by weak organismal relevance

The repository still needs clearer distinction between:
early versus structurally limited.

### 4. Which interventions are best understood as burden reduction rather than rejuvenation?

This question matters across much of the intervention section.

Many interventions may be strongest for:

- reducing inflammatory burden
- improving metabolic stability
- lowering disease-adjacent burden
- helping the organism hold better

rather than for:

- true rejuvenation
- reset
- reversal

The repository still needs stronger clarity about which interventions belong in
which category.

### 5. Which interventions are most vulnerable to biomarker overpromotion?

The intervention section already showed that some classes are especially prone
to looking stronger molecularly than they are organismally.

But the ranking is not fully settled.

The open question is:

Which intervention classes are most likely to be overpromoted because they
produce attractive biomarker movement ahead of real function?

Likely strong candidates include:

- NAD+ restoration
- plasma-exchange and circulating-factor logic
- telomere-related intervention
- some microbiome-directed interventions
- some multi-omic-followed pharmacologic strategies

This matters because these interventions may need unusually strict promotion
thresholds.

### 6. Which interventions are most likely to remain population-conditional?

Some interventions may never become broad foundation logic.

They may remain most credible in:

- high-burden adults
- disease-adjacent populations
- frailer populations
- specific metabolic states
- specific tissue- or burden-defined contexts

The repository still needs clearer answers about which intervention classes
should probably remain conditional rather than broad.

### 7. What distinguishes support-worthy interventions from exploratory ones?

This question matters for the protocol architecture directly.

Some interventions may not deserve foundation status but may still help the
foundation hold better.

The open question is:

What kind of evidence, fit, and bottleneck clarity is enough to let an
intervention function as genuine support rather than remain exploratory?

This is still unresolved for multiple classes, especially:

- narrower microbiome support
- narrower metabolic support
- selected recovery-supportive adjuncts
- some lower-risk burden-reduction strategies

### 8. When is combination logic actually stronger than single-intervention logic?

The repository already treats combination logic as important but not automatic.

What remains open is:

- which intervention pairings produce real additive functional benefit
- which combinations reduce bottleneck burden
- which combinations mainly add complexity
- which combinations are strongest because they unite biology and function,
  not because they sound comprehensive

This remains a major open intervention question.

### 9. Which interventions deserve disease-adjacent development rather than general-aging framing?

Some interventions may mature most honestly through narrower indications first.

The open question is not only:
can this intervention help aging?

It is also:
what is the narrowest context where this intervention is most biologically and
translationally credible right now?

This matters especially for:

- senolytics
- telomere-related interventions
- plasma-exchange logic
- some metabolic pharmacology
- some sleep and burden-linked targeted interventions

### 10. What would count as true threshold crossing for a currently exploratory intervention?

The repository has open questions not only about evidence amount, but about
evidence type.

Examples:

- Is one strong randomized human functional study enough?
- Is biomarker-function alignment enough if the effect is still narrow?
- Does the threshold differ by intervention type?
- Do high-risk interventions require a qualitatively stronger standard than
  lower-risk ones?

This is one of the most important unanswered questions for the next phase of
the repository.

## Highest-Priority Intervention Questions for the Repository

Not every intervention question carries equal structural weight.

The current highest-priority questions appear to be:

1. Which exploratory interventions are most likely to mature first?
2. What level of human functional evidence is enough to change intervention
   status?
3. Which interventions are structurally limited rather than merely early?
4. Which interventions belong in burden reduction rather than rejuvenation
   logic?
5. Which interventions are truly support-worthy rather than still exploratory?
6. What counts as a true threshold crossing for promotion?

These are the intervention questions most likely to affect future protocol
design.

## What Would Count As Progress on These Questions

This repository should not treat “more enthusiasm” as progress.

Progress here would mean things such as:

- stronger human functional evidence
- better fit clarity
- clearer burden-versus-rejuvenation distinction
- better alignment between biomarkers and organismal outcomes
- stronger translation in the intended population
- lower or better-bounded risk
- clearer reasons for promotion or continued nonpromotion

That is the kind of progress this section should track.

## Intervention Failure Modes This File Should Prevent

This file should help prevent several common failures:

### Failure Mode 1 | Quiet promotion

An intervention remains officially exploratory while informally being treated
as support or near-core.

### Failure Mode 2 | Evidence-type confusion

A biomarker, mechanistic, disease-adjacent, or pilot-scale signal is treated as
if it were functionally dispositive.

### Failure Mode 3 | Reversal inflation

Burden reduction or support effects are spoken about as if they already amount
to rejuvenation.

### Failure Mode 4 | Structural naivety

An intervention is treated as merely early when the limitation may actually be
built into the mechanism.

### Failure Mode 5 | Combination fantasy

Stacking and synergy are spoken about as if they were the same thing.
