# 01_HALLMARKS

This section tracks the major hallmarks of biological aging and the current understanding of how they interact.

The hallmarks are not isolated categories. They operate as a connected system. Damage or dysfunction in one area often amplifies failure in others. That compounding interaction is part of why aging appears to accelerate over time rather than progress at a fixed rate.

This section is not meant to treat all hallmarks as equally upstream, equally causal, or equally tractable. One of the core research questions in this repository is whether some hallmarks are better understood as drivers, others as downstream consequences, and others as feedback amplifiers.

## Current Hallmark Set

This repository tracks the following recognized hallmarks of aging:

1. Genomic instability
2. Telomere attrition
3. Epigenetic alterations
4. Loss of proteostasis
5. Disabled macroautophagy
6. Deregulated nutrient sensing
7. Mitochondrial dysfunction
8. Cellular senescence
9. Stem cell exhaustion
10. Altered intercellular communication
11. Chronic inflammation
12. Dysbiosis

## Working View

The hallmarks matter individually, but the more important problem is how they interact.

Aging may not be one thing. It may be a system of co-amplifying failures with some mechanisms acting more upstream than others.

This section is built to support that question, not collapse it too early.

## What This Section Tracks

For each hallmark, the goal is to document:

- core mechanism
- why it matters
- whether it appears upstream, downstream, or mixed
- known interactions with other hallmarks
- candidate interventions relevant to that hallmark
- biomarker options, if any
- major uncertainties or disputes

## Current Research Questions in This Section

- Are some hallmarks primary drivers while others are mostly downstream effects?
- Which hallmarks are most actionable with current intervention strategies?
- Which hallmark interactions appear most important in human aging?
- Are there a small number of leverage points that affect multiple hallmarks at once?
- Which hallmarks are best tracked with existing biomarkers, and which remain difficult to measure well?
