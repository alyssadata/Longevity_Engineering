# 00_INDEX

Longevity Engineering is an open research framework on biological aging, longevity, and reversal hypotheses.

It is designed to track the field clearly, separate evidence from hype, and organize candidate interventions by mechanism, evidence maturity, and risk.

This is not a treatment guide.  
It is not a clinical protocol.  
It is not anti-aging marketing.

It is a structured research system.

## Core Aim

The aim of this repository is to identify which upstream aging mechanisms matter most, which intervention directions appear most credible, and where the strongest open questions still remain.

The central problem is not simply how to extend lifespan.

The real question is whether biological aging can be understood well enough to slow, interrupt, or reverse aging processes without creating greater risk than benefit.

## Research Standard

This repository separates:

- established framework
- early human evidence
- strong animal evidence
- mechanistic speculation
- hype

No intervention is treated as validated for human aging unless credible human evidence supports that claim.

If the science moves, the repo moves with it.

## Repository Structure

### `/01_HALLMARKS`
Tracks the major hallmarks of aging and the current understanding of how they interact.

### `/02_BIOMARKERS`
Tracks clocks, lab markers, functional measures, and other candidate ways to measure biological aging or intervention response.

### `/03_INTERVENTIONS`
Tracks candidate interventions by mechanism, evidence level, and relevance to specific hallmarks.

### `/04_RISKS_AND_FAILURE_MODES`
Tracks tradeoffs, safety concerns, oncogenic risk, metabolic risk, immune risk, and false-signal problems in the field.

### `/05_PROTOCOL_DESIGN`
Explores whether a minimum viable longevity framework can be built for an adult prioritizing cognition, physical capacity, and long healthspan.

### `/06_OPEN_QUESTIONS`
Holds unresolved questions that should not be collapsed into premature answers.

### `/07_SOURCES`
Maintains the source base for papers, reviews, trials, and evidence summaries.

### `/08_NOTES`
Working notes, synthesis drafts, idea fragments, and directional thinking.

## Starting Priorities

Current high-priority research tracks:

1. Partial epigenetic reprogramming
2. Senolytic clearance
3. mTOR modulation and nutrient sensing
4. Biomarker validity
5. Risk boundaries for combination approaches

## Guiding Questions

- Is aging driven primarily by one upstream mechanism or by interacting hallmarks?
- Which interventions have the strongest human-relevant evidence?
- Which theories are structurally important but still too early for strong claims?
- Where does possible reversal stop and unacceptable risk begin?
- What does a credible longevity framework look like if the priority is healthspan, cognition, and function rather than fantasy?

## Status

Active research. No settled conclusions.

This repository is meant to become more exact over time, not more inflated.
